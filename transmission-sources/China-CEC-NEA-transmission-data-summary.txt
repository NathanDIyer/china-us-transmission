Notes on Ditan chart data:
1. Additions (增量) do NOT equal cumulative differences — the gap reflects statistical
   revisions, voltage reclassifications, and line retirements/decommissions.
   Example: 2020 additions = 32,000 km but cumulative grew by 53,000 km (from 759k to 812k).
2. The 2020 value of 812,000 km differs from CEC 2020 preliminary stats (794,118 km).
   The Ditan report was published in 2024 using revised/final CEC data, while 794,118 was
   the preliminary figure from early 2021. The revised figure (812,000) should be used.
3. Text on page 60 gives exact 2023 figure: "220千伏及以上输电线路回路长度共92.05万千米"
   = 920,500 km (+4.3% YoY). This is more precise than the chart's rounded 92.0万千米.
4. 2024 figure from CEC Annual Development Report 2025: exact 960,970 km.
5. 14th FYP trend: "220千伏及以上输电线路回路长度增速维持在4%上下" (~4% annual growth)

Key observations:

- Grid investment declined through 2019 (485.6B) then slowly recovered
- Generation investment first exceeded grid investment in 2020 (529B vs 490B)
- By 2023, generation was 440B more than grid (968B vs 528B)

- In 2024, grid investment surged +15.3% to 608.4B, its highest ever

=== SGCC (State Grid Corporation of China) ===

SGCC publishes annual sustainability/CSR reports at sgcc.com.cn and en.sgcc.com.cn.
These reports contain transmission line construction data but were not independently
downloaded due to website access limitations. SGCC operates ~88% of China's grid
(excluding China Southern Power Grid territory in 5 southern provinces).

The China Southern Power Grid (CSG) publishes similar annual reports at csg.cn.

=== DASHBOARD VERIFICATION ===

The dashboard uses ~365,000 miles for China cumulative high-voltage transmission.
Converting: 920,000 km × 0.621371 = ~571,700 miles (220kV+)
But the dashboard's 365,000 figure likely uses a higher voltage threshold or different methodology.

The key comparison metric is the RATIO and annual construction pace, not absolute levels,
since China and US use different voltage standards and counting methods.

=== NOTES ===

1. CEC data is published annually in January/February for the previous year
2. NEA publishes monthly statistics ~20th of each month
3. chinaenergyportal.org stopped adding new years after 2020
4. CEIC Data (ceicdata.com) aggregates CEC/NBS data but requires subscription for detailed access
5. The CET programme publishes annual English summaries focused on generation/consumption
6. Full voltage-class breakdown is only available in the "detailed" (详细) version of CEC stats,
   not the "preliminary" (快报) version which only shows 220kV+ totals
7. The CEC Annual Development Report (年度发展报告) is published each July, covering the prior year
8. Post-2020 voltage-class breakdowns not available in any free source (English or Chinese);
   the CEC detailed statistics are behind a paywall/membership
9. Credit rating agency reports (联合资信, etc.) sometimes republish CEC aggregate data
   in their industry analysis sections, accessible via dfcfw.com and lhratings.com
